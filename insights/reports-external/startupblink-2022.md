# StartupBlink Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2022

## TL;DR
Canada maintains #4 global ranking. The report introduces sectoral breakdowns (fintech, healthtech, AI, cleantech, Web3). Interest rate environment and funding market correction begin impacting dynamics. Montreal recognized for emerging strengths in AI, healthtech, and cleantech. Emerging focus on sustainability and impact-driven startups across ecosystems.

## Key Data Points

1. **[entrepreneurship]** Canada ranks #4 globally (consistent with 2020).
2. **[macro-trends]** Separate sectoral rankings introduced for fintech, healthtech, AI, cleantech, Web3.
3. **[funding]** Rising interest rates and VC correction beginning to impact allocation.
4. **[macro-trends]** Ecosystem uncertainty reflected in more cautious capital deployment.
5. **[ai-sector]** Cross-sector collaboration and startup pivots in response to market signals.
6. **[entrepreneurship]** Canadian startups increasing international revenue and geographic diversification.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

- Montreal emerging strengths in healthtech, cleantech, and AI specialization
- Continued government support through funding mechanisms and accelerator programs
- Montreal recognized for AI talent and research concentration
- COVID-catalyzed healthtech sector continues developing with sustainable business models
- Quebec startups increasingly accessing US venture capital markets

## Methodology & Limitations

- Core three-pillar methodology with sectoral sub-indexes
- New "Contender Ecosystems" category for emerging high-growth ecosystems
- **Limitations:** Sectoral data varies by region; macro-economic volatility increases uncertainty; Web3 data quality questions

## Relevance to Our Work

Sectoral focus particularly relevant for Quebec where healthtech, AI, and cleantech represent competitive advantages. "Contender" status framework useful for assessing Quebec trajectory.
