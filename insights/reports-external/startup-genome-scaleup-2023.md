# The Scaleup Report 2023

## TL;DR
Startup Genome's Scaleup Report provides longitudinal research on what differentiates early-stage startups that successfully scale. Based on 11 years of primary research tracking nearly 100,000 founders globally and 3.5 million companies across 290+ ecosystems, the report finds that only 4.6% of startups achieve scaleup status ($50M+ valuation). Scaleup rates increase significantly in mature ecosystems. Includes a dedicated Quebec section: "Hypercrissance Québec."

## Key Data Points

1. **[entrepreneurship]** Only 4.6% of startups achieve scaleup status (USD $50M+ valuation).
2. **[macro-trends]** 3.5 million companies studied across 290+ startup ecosystems.
3. **[entrepreneurship]** Scaleup rates by ecosystem lifecycle phase: Activation (4.6%), Attraction (7.1%), Globalization (9.5%), Integration (13.1%).
4. **[macro-trends]** Silicon Valley comprises 9% of all scaleups globally.
5. **[entrepreneurship]** Local Connectedness Index (LCI) measuring network density strongly correlates with scaleup success.
6. **[funding]** 60+ billion-dollar founders researched across 80+ countries.
7. **[macro-trends]** Ecosystem lifecycle has five phases: Activation, Attraction, Globalization, Integration, Resource Leakage.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

- Dedicated section: "Hypercrissance Québec: Empowering Home-Grown Startups to Scale Beyond Borders" (page 58)
- Quebec ecosystem positioned as case study for scaleup expansion beyond regional boundaries
- Ecosystem lifecycle phases applied specifically to Quebec context
- Quebec identified as capable of scaling home-grown startups through focused strategy

## Methodology & Limitations

- Longitudinal study spanning 11+ years of founder and company tracking
- Machine-learning techniques to clean and validate 3.5M company dataset
- Combines data from Crunchbase, Dealroom, PitchBook with proprietary databases
- **Limitations:** Focused on tech scaleups; does not cover non-tech ecosystems at equal depth
