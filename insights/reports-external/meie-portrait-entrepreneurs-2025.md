# Les entrepreneurs du Québec en chiffres: Portrait sociodémographique - Édition 2025

## TL;DR
Quebec's entrepreneur population is aging and declining, with stabilization at approximately 128,000 entrepreneurs as of 2024, significantly lower than the 197,000 peak in 1992. The proportion of female entrepreneurs remains below the Canadian average, and immigrant entrepreneurs show lower entrepreneurship rates than in the rest of Canada, representing key talent and diversification gaps for the province's startup ecosystem.

## Key Data Points

1. **[macro-trends]** Entrepreneur population in Quebec stabilized at approximately 128,000 in 2023-2024 after declining from 197,000 in 1992.
2. **[entrepreneurship]** Entrepreneurship rate in Quebec declined to 1.8% in 2024, down from 2.1% in 1977, tracking consistently below the rest of Canada's 2.3% rate.
3. **[macro-trends]** New entrepreneur rate in Quebec fell to 3.7% in 2024, declining from a peak of 10.8% in 1980, parallel to broader Canadian decline to 3.7%.
4. **[entrepreneurship]** Entrepreneurs per 1,000 enterprises in Quebec dropped to 553 in 2024 from 912 in 2001, reflecting fundamental shift in business structure with fewer per-business entrepreneurs.
5. **[talent]** Female entrepreneurs represent 24.8% of Quebec's entrepreneurial population in 2024, slightly below the Canadian average of 26.6%, up from 10.5% in 1978.
6. **[talent]** Average age of Quebec entrepreneurs is 49.8 years in 2024, nearly reaching the Canadian average of 50.0 years, up from 42.9 years in 1977.
7. **[talent]** Entrepreneurs aged 55 and older comprise 37.4% of Quebec's entrepreneurial population in 2024, up from 16.1% in 1978, indicating significant aging of the workforce.
8. **[talent]** Proportion of entrepreneurs under 40 years old in Quebec is 24.1% in 2024, remaining stable since 2018 at approximately 23-24%.
9. **[entrepreneurship]** Entrepreneurs with tertiary education (post-secondary diploma or university) represent 71.7% of Quebec's population in 2024, up from 47.2% in 1991.
10. **[talent]** Immigrant entrepreneurs in Quebec show 1.8% entrepreneurship rate (Canadian-born: 2.3%), while immigrants admitted 10+ years show 2.2% versus rest of Canada's 3.2%.
11. **[entrepreneurship]** Montreal metropolitan area has the lowest entrepreneurship rate (1.8%) among major Canadian metropolitan regions, well below Vancouver (2.9%), Toronto (2.7%), and Edmonton (2.5%).
12. **[entrepreneurship]** Service sector entrepreneurs comprise 70.6% of Quebec's entrepreneurial population in 2024, consistent with rest of Canada's 68.7%.
13. **[entrepreneurship]** Proportion of entrepreneurs with multiple employees has grown to 6.4% in Quebec by 2024, up from 2.8% in 1980, indicating slight shift toward job-creating enterprises.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

- Quebec's entrepreneurship ecosystem shows persistent underperformance compared to the rest of Canada across multiple dimensions: total entrepreneur count, entrepreneurship rate, new entrepreneur rate, female participation, and immigrant entrepreneurship rates.
- The Montreal region has the lowest entrepreneurship rate (1.8%) among major metropolitan regions in Canada, suggesting urban concentration effects differ from other Canadian cities.
- Quebec's aging entrepreneur population (49.8 years average age) is at parity with Canada overall, but the proportion of entrepreneurs 55+ has nearly doubled since 1978 (16.1% to 37.4%), creating potential talent renewal challenges.
- The growing proportion of highly educated entrepreneurs (71.7% with post-secondary credentials in 2024, up from 47.2% in 1991) may reflect broader tertiary education expansion and/or shifting skill requirements for business viability.
- Despite female entrepreneurship growth from 10.5% (1978) to 24.8% (2024), a 14.3 percentage-point gap remains versus the rest of Canada (26.6%).
- Immigrant entrepreneurship rates in Quebec (1.8-2.2%) lag significantly behind rest of Canada (3.2% for 10+ year residents), suggesting integration or systemic barriers to immigrant business formation.

## Methodology & Limitations

**Data Source:** Enquête sur la population active (EPA - Labour Force Survey) and Programme d'analyse longitudinale de l'emploi (PALE) from Statistics Canada.

**Definition of Entrepreneurs:** Individuals identified by EPA as self-employed workers with employees, or self-employed workers without employees.

**Measurement Methods:**
- 3-year and 5-year moving averages applied to smooth variations caused by EPA sampling fluctuations
- Data for 2023-2024 are provisional estimates

**Exclusions and Limitations:**
- Data do not capture informal or subsistence entrepreneurship below official statistics thresholds
- Estimates use moving averages, which attenuate year-to-year volatility but may mask short-term fluctuations

**Compilation:** Direction générale des politiques, de l'analyse économique et de l'achat local (MEIE), ISBN 978-2-555-00599-0
