# BDC Canada's Venture Capital Landscape 2025

## TL;DR
Canadian VC investment reached $7.9B across 592 deals in 2024 (+10% YoY), with AI capturing a record 30% of capital. However, zero VC-backed IPOs persisted and median exit values ($30M) lagged the US significantly. Quebec VC growth stalled at 0.3% five-year CAGR due to weak exit activity, while life sciences emerged as the province's sector differentiator at 36% of VC investment.

## Key Data Points
1. **Canadian VC Investment (2024):** $7.9B across 592 deals (+10% YoY, −13.6% deal count)
2. **Average Deal Size:** $13.3M (+27% YoY)
3. **Largest Deal:** Clio at $1.24B—largest round in Canadian tech history
4. **VC-to-GDP Ratio:** 0.35%, ranking 6th among OECD nations
5. **AI Sector Share:** 30% of all Canadian VC (new all-time high)
6. **IPO Market:** Zero VC-backed IPOs in 2024 (drought since 2022)
7. **Median Exit Value:** $30M (vs. $232.7M in US)
8. **10-Year Net IRR:** Canada 10%, with US gap widening >450 bps
9. **Foreign Investor Participation:** 78% of VC dollars but only 39% of deals
10. **Dry Powder:** $11.5B held by Canadian VCs
11. **Quebec VC Share:** 25% of national VC dollars ($1.859B)
12. **Quebec 5-Year CAGR:** 0.3% (essentially flat)
13. **Quebec Life Sciences Share:** 36% of provincial VC (double the 18% national share)
14. **Quebec 10-Year IRRs:** Life Sciences 23.6%, ICT 15.9%, ECT 10.9%

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Quebec captured 25% of national VC dollars ($1.859B) in 2024
- Five-year VC CAGR was essentially flat at 0.3%, significantly lagging Prairies (40%), BC (28%), and Ontario (13%)
- Life sciences accounted for 36% of Quebec VC investment (double the 18% national share)
- Weak exit activity is the primary driver of Quebec's stalled growth, creating capital recycling constraints
- 10-year gross IRR by sector: Life Sciences 23.6%, ICT 15.9%, ECT 10.9%

## Methodology & Limitations
- Based on CVCA year-end 2024 data, BDC proprietary fund data, PitchBook, and Crunchbase
- BDC improved IRR methodology in 2025 edition; data not directly comparable to prior editions
- Excludes PE funds, private debt, funds of funds, government funds, and accelerators
