# Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2020

## TL;DR
The 2020 GSER examined startup ecosystems during COVID-19's emergence, documenting 80+ billion-dollar ecosystems globally with cumulative value exceeding $2.6 trillion. Venture rounds dropped 17% in Q1 2020 as the pandemic struck. The report emphasizes policy's critical role in recovery and resilience, and documents that four out of ten startups had 3 months or fewer of capital runway.

## Key Data Points

1. **[funding]** Over $300 billion in venture capital invested in 2019; venture rounds dropped 17% in Q1 2020 as COVID struck.
2. **[macro-trends]** Over 80 ecosystems globally have produced unicorns or billion-dollar exits; cumulative value exceeds $2.6 trillion.
3. **[exits]** 20% year-over-year growth in dollar value of startup exits globally (pre-pandemic trajectory).
4. **[talent]** Only 14.1% of founders globally are female; value creation concentrated in top 10 performing cities.
5. **[funding]** Four out of ten startups have 3 months or fewer of capital runway at pandemic onset.
6. **[talent]** Startups laid off average 33% of staff in early 2020.
7. **[policy]** Policy support identified as critical to startup survival and economic recovery post-COVID.
