# Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2018

## TL;DR
The 2018 GSER advances the ecosystem framework with updated global rankings and emphasis on deliberate ecosystem strategy — how targeted government and institutional interventions can accelerate transitions between lifecycle stages. Major ecosystems increasingly specialize in particular sectors (AI, fintech, biotech) rather than pursuing generic development.

## Key Data Points

1. **[entrepreneurship]** Updated rankings for 30+ ecosystems with refined metrics on momentum and consolidation.
2. **[policy]** Deliberate strategic interventions by government and institutions accelerate ecosystem development; passive approaches yield slower progress.
3. **[macro-trends]** Major ecosystems increasingly specialize in particular sectors (AI, fintech, biotech) rather than generic "startup ecosystem" development.
4. **[funding]** Government-backed venture funds, innovation grants, and regulatory support have become critical infrastructure in scaling emerging ecosystems.
5. **[talent]** Ecosystems that attract and retain top technical talent experience accelerated growth; universities serve as talent anchors.
6. **[incubation]** Incubators, accelerators, and shared workspaces provide essential scaffolding for early-stage companies in opening-stage ecosystems.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

No detailed Quebec-specific analysis in the report's key sections. The emphasis on deliberate ecosystem strategy and sub-sector specialization suggests frameworks applicable to Montreal's competitive positioning.

- Builds on 2017 methodology with updated data collection and refined analytical categories
