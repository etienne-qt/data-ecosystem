# Statistics Canada: Survey on Financing and Growth of SMEs, 2023

## TL;DR
Large-scale Statistics Canada survey (22,084 respondents, 52% response rate) reveals a financing chasm in Canada's SME ecosystem: only 1% of SMEs sought equity financing despite 49.3% requesting external capital (returning to pre-pandemic norm). Technology adoption outpaces innovation output: 51% adopted new technology but only 27% introduced any innovation in 2021–2023. Intellectual property remains rare (15% hold any IP), with patents particularly limited (2%).

## Key Data Points
1. **External financing requests:** 49.3% of SMEs (returning to 47.1% pre-pandemic norm)
2. **Equity financing:** Only 1% of SMEs requested equity (vs. 26% debt, 27% trade credit)
3. **Total debt requests:** $94.0B
4. **Bank lending:** Chartered banks provided 68.5% of debt
5. **Innovation adoption:** Only 27% introduced any innovation (2021–2023)
6. **Technology adoption:** 51% adopted at least one new technology
7. **Intellectual property:** 15% hold any IP (NDAs 9%, trademarks 7%, patents 2%)
8. **Ownership diversity:** 65% male-owned, 17% female-owned
9. **Top obstacles:** Rising input costs (78.9%), corporate tax rate (72.1%), competition (71.0%)

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Quebec SMEs subject to same national financing patterns (survey is Canada-wide)
- Rising input costs (78.9% citing) directly impacts Quebec manufacturers and service sectors
- Corporate tax rate burden (72.1%) particularly relevant given Quebec provincial tax landscape
- Female entrepreneurship represented at 17% of SMEs surveyed, indicating gender gap in business ownership

## Methodology & Limitations
- Survey instrument: Statistics Canada with consortium support (ISED-led)
- Sample size: 22,084 respondents
- Response rate: 52%
- Time period: Reference year 2023; published February 2025
- Full summary accessible (The Daily release + ISED summary); detailed data tables available separately
- Credibility: High (Statistics Canada official statistics)
