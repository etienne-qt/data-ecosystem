# Réseau Capital Year-End 2024

## TL;DR
Quebec's venture capital market contracted sharply in 2024 with $2.0B across 108 deals (25% decline in volume but 35% increase in amounts), while private equity soared to a record $19.07B. Quebec leads Canada in AI VC investment share but faces a critical scaleup gap, with only 14% of Canadian scaleups despite generating 22% of new startups. Early 2025 data shows a 55% YoY funding decline, signaling macro headwinds.

## Key Data Points
1. **Quebec VC 2024** — $2.0B across 108 deals (−25% volume, +35% amount vs. 2023)
2. **Quebec VC seed stage 2024** — 39 deals/$112M (−52% volume, −65% amounts)
3. **Quebec VC IT/ICT sector** — 58 deals/$1.02B (54% of deals, 51% of amounts)
4. **Quebec VC exits 2024** — Zero publicly disclosed exits
5. **Quebec PE 2024** — $19.07B across 385 deals (highest since 2013; 59% of Canadian PE deals, 69% of PE value)
6. **Quebec AI VC deal share (2019–2024)** — 24% of Canadian AI deals (1st nationally); 28% at seed stage
7. **Quebec startup-to-scaleup ratio** — 22% of new Canadian startups but only 14% of scaleups
8. **Quebec scaleups exceeding $10M revenue (past decade)** — ~20 firms (vs. 150+ nationally)
9. **Quebec VC through Q3 2025** — $676M across 85 deals (−55% YoY)
10. **Quebec VC seed growth Q3 2025** — Only stage showing growth at +31% YoY

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Quebec leads Canada in AI VC deal concentration (24% of national AI deals 2019–2024) with particular strength at seed/angel stage (28%)
- The scaleup gap is acute: Quebec generates 22% of Canadian startups but produces only 14% of scaleups; only ~20 Quebec companies exceeded $10M revenue in a decade versus 150+ nationally
- Montreal remains the primary investment hub for Quebec capital
- Report introduced three new tracking dimensions: AI Index, International Attractiveness Index, and scaleup metrics
- Seed-stage funding entered sharp downturn in 2025 (−52% in 2024, but Q3 2025 showed +31% growth signaling potential pipeline recovery)
- Zero VC exits in 2024 represents a complete absence of public liquidity events
