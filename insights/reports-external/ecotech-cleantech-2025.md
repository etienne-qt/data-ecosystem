# Écotech Québec — Cartographie du financement des technologies propres au Québec

## TL;DR
Quebec's cleantech sector comprises 400+ innovative companies and 85,000 jobs, grounded in the province's hydroelectric heritage. While Canada ranks 2nd globally for cleantech attractiveness and 77% of Canadian cleantech exports target the US, Quebec is underrepresented in seed and demonstration-stage VC relative to Ontario and BC. This mapping report identifies funding gaps as major barriers to ecosystem growth, especially following the end of SDTC/TDDC federal support.

## Key Data Points
1. **Sector Scale** – 400+ cleantech companies; 85,000 jobs in Quebec
2. **Global Position** – Canada ranks 2nd globally for cleantech attractiveness; 77% of Canadian cleantech exports go to US
3. **Funding Gap** – Quebec underrepresented in cleantech seed/demo-stage VC vs. Ontario and BC
4. **Policy Framework** – Quebec GHG target: 37.5% reduction by 2030; carbon neutrality by 2050
5. **Recent Capital** – Cycle Capital Fund IV: $145M dedicated to cleantech; BDC Fonds Avenir durable: $150M for sustainable ventures
6. **Ecosystem Challenge** – End of SDTC/TDDC funding has weakened Quebec's cleantech ecosystem

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Cleantech represents a major employment pillar (85,000 jobs) comparable to aerospace, leveraging Quebec's unique hydroelectric advantage
- Significant funding gap at critical early-stage and demonstration phases, limiting scaling from innovation to commercialization
- Federal funding withdrawal (SDTC/TDDC) has reduced ecosystem support capacity
- Policy alignment through official GHG targets creates demand-side incentives for cleantech solutions

## Methodology & Limitations
- Sourced from Écotech Québec, Quebec's official cleantech cluster organization; first-of-its-kind comprehensive mapping
- Full report gated; announcement page and pre-budget submission reviewed
- Funding metrics reflect reported allocations; actual deployment may vary
- Data circa May 2025
