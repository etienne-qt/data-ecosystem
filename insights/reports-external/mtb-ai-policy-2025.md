# The Calm Before the AI Storm: Global Innovation Ecosystems

## TL;DR
The global startup economy has reached $4.2 trillion in capital raised across 97,982 scaleups, yet innovation remains dramatically concentrated in North America (43% of scaleups, 50% of capital) and Asia-Pacific, with Europe and emerging regions significantly underrepresented. Quebec appears as an emerging growth hub (Late Startup stage, 216 scaleups) that has been accelerating notably between 2022-2024, positioning itself among the fastest-moving North American ecosystems.

## Key Data Points

1. **[macro-trends]** $4.2 trillion in venture capital invested over 25 years creating 97,982 scaleups globally as of 2025.
2. **[macro-trends]** Only 14 countries have developed scaleup ecosystems large enough for global competitiveness; North America dominates with 43% of scaleups and 50% of capital raised.
3. **[funding]** Europe has 22% of scaleups but only 13% of global investments, indicating structural funding gaps.
4. **[entrepreneurship]** Nearly 900 ecosystems now populate the global curve (up from 500 in 2015), with 64 in late stages of development.
5. **[entrepreneurship]** Silicon Valley (11,329 scaleups) leads globally, followed by New York City (6,023) and London (3,933).
6. **[macro-trends]** Star-stage ecosystems generate ~500 new scaleups annually vs. barely 25 in Startup stage.
7. **[macro-trends]** Average time to progress through ecosystem life cycle is ~4 years per sub-phase, with total progression from Standup to Star taking 25-30 years.
8. **[entrepreneurship]** São Paulo, Gyeonggi, Quebec, Alberta, Riyadh, Sichuan, and Mexico City are advancing fastest through life-cycle stages (2022-2024).
9. **[policy]** Nearly two-thirds of leading ecosystem builders have advanced 8-10 policy initiatives per year over the past three years, with 33% promoting 11+ annually.
10. **[commercialization]** Industry support evolving from networking (95%) toward venture client facilitation (73%), reflecting strategic shift toward structured corporate-startup collaboration.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

- Quebec appears as a Late Startup stage ecosystem with 216 scaleups, advancing notably between 2022-2024.
- Listed among the fastest-moving North American ecosystems outside major tech centers.
- Positioned for potential transition to Scaleup stage (300+ scaleups) if current trajectory continues.

## Methodology & Limitations

- Data derives from Crunchbase and analysis by Mind the Bridge in partnership with ICC.
- Framework categorizes ecosystems into four stages: Standup (0-75 scaleups), Startup (75-300), Scaleup (300-1,200), Star (1,200+).
- Tracks 14 leading countries and 50+ global innovation regions.
- Most innovation policies are local (cities/regions), making city-level analysis essential.

- **Policy framework:** Six core growth drivers (startup support, industry engagement, talent & research, investment boost, public procurement, innovation policy) offer actionable policy assessment framework
