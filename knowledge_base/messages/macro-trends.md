## GenAI Impact on Company Formation

### MSG-MACRO-01: Tech product creation has doubled post-GenAI
- **Claim:** Tech product company incorporations in Quebec rose from 34/month (2017–2019 baseline) to 76/month in 2025–early 2026, a 2.2x increase — with a Difference-in-Differences analysis showing +27.7 percentage points of excess growth relative to the non-tech economy.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-genai-impact-req-2026
- **Implication:** The GenAI era has materially changed the rate at which Quebec founders incorporate product companies — QT's registry and annual report should incorporate this formation acceleration into its narrative about ecosystem vitality.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-MACRO-02: GenAI effect was delayed 18 months
- **Claim:** Tech product company creation was flat (39/month) in the "Early GenAI" period (H2 2022–2023), consistent with the baseline of 34/month, before accelerating sharply in 2024 (44/month) and 2025 (76/month) — suggesting an approximately 18-month lag between GenAI tool availability and measurable incorporation behavior.
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-genai-impact-req-2026
- **Implication:** The current acceleration in 2025 is likely the leading edge of a sustained wave, not a peak — QT should plan for continued ecosystem growth in its 2026 data collection and reporting capacity.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-MACRO-03: AI/ML companies have quadrupled since pre-GenAI
- **Claim:** Quebec companies explicitly mentioning artificial intelligence, machine learning, or related terms in their REQ registration description grew from 10–28 per quarter in 2019 to 50–71 per quarter in 2025, with Q1 2026 at 54/quarter — roughly a 4x increase.
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-genai-impact-req-2026
- **Implication:** AI/ML is no longer a niche sub-segment — it is the dominant growth vector in new Quebec tech incorporation; any sector analysis that does not separate AI-native from non-AI tech companies will miss the most important compositional shift of this decade.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-MACRO-04: SaaS company creation surged 6–8x post-2023
- **Claim:** Quebec companies mentioning "SaaS" in their REQ registration grew from approximately 3/quarter before 2023 to 19–26/quarter in late 2025–early 2026, a roughly 6–8x increase over the same period.
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-genai-impact-req-2026
- **Implication:** The SaaS model has become the dominant incorporation template for new Quebec tech ventures; funders and programs calibrated to traditional software or services models should update their assessment criteria.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

## Company Size & Composition

### MSG-MACRO-05: Micro-companies dominate — 97% zero-employee post-GenAI
- **Claim:** Among tech product companies incorporated since the GenAI era, 97% report zero employees at registration, up from 88% in the 2017–2019 baseline — consistent with GenAI enabling solo founders or very small teams to build products that previously required larger teams.
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-genai-impact-req-2026
- **Implication:** The new generation of Quebec tech startups skews heavily toward micro-ventures; support programs calibrated for 5–20 employee teams at formation (e.g., traditional accelerator cohort models) may be misaligned with how founders are actually starting companies in 2025–2026.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

## Economy-Wide Context

### MSG-MACRO-06: Software dev and consulting flat — GenAI effect is product-only
- **Claim:** Software development firms (76–89/month throughout 2017–2026) and tech-adjacent consultancies (90–106/month) show no meaningful formation growth in the GenAI era, while tech product companies have doubled — with the DiD analysis showing consulting and dev firms underperforming non-tech by 6–7 percentage points.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-genai-impact-req-2026
- **Implication:** GenAI is selectively amplifying product company formation while leaving service-firm formation unchanged; this may signal that AI tools are enabling direct product creation while partially displacing the need for standalone IT consulting — a structural shift in how the tech sector generates economic value.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15
