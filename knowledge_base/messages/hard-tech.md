## Formation Rates

### MSG-HARDTECH-01: Hard-tech incorporation rate — 2025 uptick
- **Claim:** Hardware/photonics company incorporations in Quebec have risen to approximately 18.2 per quarter in 2025–early 2026, up ~50% from the pre-COVID baseline of 12.1/quarter (2017–2019), though the absolute numbers remain small and the trend is preliminary.
- **Confidence:** Low
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-hardware-photonics-req-2026
- **Implication:** There is a potential early signal of hard-tech formation acceleration in Quebec, but the absolute counts are too small (single-digit quarterly sub-categories) to make definitive claims — this warrants monitoring over 2–3 more quarters before inclusion in public reports.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-HARDTECH-02: Photonics core is flat — growth is in adjacent hardware
- **Claim:** Pure photonics company creation (laser, fiber optic, optoelectronic companies) has held flat at ~2.0–2.2 per quarter throughout 2017–2026; all recent growth is concentrated in the broader "adjacent hardware" sub-category (semiconductors, sensors, quantum) and "optics & imaging."
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-hardware-photonics-req-2026
- **Implication:** Quebec's photonics identity (INO, Exfo, II-VI) is not generating a new wave of photonics startups — the sector's commercial spinoff activity may be occurring through channels not captured by REQ incorporations (federal registration, stealth operations).
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

## Sector Comparisons

### MSG-HARDTECH-03: Hard-tech vs software — formation contrast post-2022
- **Claim:** While tech product/software company creation surged 2.2x post-2022 (GenAI era), hardware/photonics formation dipped during the same period (2022–2023) before recovering in 2024–2025, confirming that the GenAI formation wave is software-first and hardware has not yet experienced a comparable inflection.
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-hardware-photonics-req-2026, INTERNAL-genai-impact-req-2026
- **Implication:** Policy targeting hard-tech formation (capital incentives, CHIPS Act response, deep-tech accelerators) should not assume it will benefit automatically from the GenAI tailwind — hard-tech requires dedicated instruments.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

## Data Quality Caveats

### MSG-HARDTECH-04: REQ hard-tech signal is noisy — 73% CAE fallback
- **Claim:** 73% of the 547 hardware/photonics companies identified in the REQ rely on CAE sector code classification (not self-described keywords), meaning the majority did not register using photonics or hardware terminology — limiting the analytical confidence of any formation trend derived from this data.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-hardware-photonics-req-2026
- **Implication:** Any public claim about Quebec's hard-tech sector size should note the REQ data quality limitation; INO, MEDTEQ, and sector association membership data would provide a more reliable signal for research reports.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-HARDTECH-05: False-positive exclusion reveals beauty-laser as dominant noise
- **Claim:** The REQ hard-tech analysis required exclusion of 3,757 false-positive companies — predominantly beauty clinics (~2,500+) and laser cutting/engraving services — confirming that "photonics" and "laser" terms in Quebec registrations are dominated by non-tech uses.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-hardware-photonics-req-2026
- **Implication:** Any automated keyword scan of REQ data for hard-tech signals must include robust exclusion filters; unfiltered counts would overstate the photonics sector by 7–8x.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15
