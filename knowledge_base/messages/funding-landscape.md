## Deal Volume & Size

### MSG-FUNDING-01: Quebec deal count vs peer provinces
- **Claim:** Quebec averages ~21 VC deals per year (2020–2025), compared to Ontario (76), British Columbia (55), and Alberta (26) — roughly 3x fewer deals than Ontario despite being Canada's second-largest province by population and GDP.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-sprint1-funding-2026
- **Implication:** Quebec's thin deal pipeline is the single most important structural fact in the ecosystem — every policy recommendation on capital formation should lead with this number.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-FUNDING-02: Quebec deal size — largest among big four
- **Claim:** Quebec's average VC deal size is $5.3M CAD (2020–2025), the highest among Canada's four largest provinces (Ontario $3.2M, BC $3.7M, Alberta $4.9M), suggesting a concentration model where fewer companies receive larger checks.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-sprint1-funding-2026
- **Implication:** The high average deal size is not a sign of strength — it reflects a state-capital-dominated model where institutional investors make fewer but larger bets, leaving most startups without access to capital.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-FUNDING-03: Quebec missed the 2021 VC boom
- **Claim:** In 2021, Quebec raised only $43M CAD across 26 VC deals while Ontario raised $417M (96 deals) and BC raised $436M (89 deals) — Quebec failed to capture the global VC boom that defined that year for every other major Canadian ecosystem.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-sprint1-funding-2026
- **Implication:** The structural isolation from US/international capital was not a recent phenomenon — Quebec was already disconnected during the peak funding environment of 2020–2022.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

## Stage Distribution

### MSG-FUNDING-04: Early Stage VC gap — near-absence of Series A
- **Claim:** Quebec had 0–3 Early Stage VC deals per year from 2020–2025 (total: 12 deals, $91.9M), compared to Ontario's 5–13 per year, indicating that Series A/B equivalent funding is functionally unavailable for most Quebec startups.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-sprint1-funding-2026
- **Implication:** This is the most actionable finding for policy: the problem is not Seed funding (40+ deals/year) or Later Stage (some large rounds) — it is the missing bridge between them, the "Series A desert."
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-FUNDING-05: Later Stage concentration — top 23 deals hold 59% of capital
- **Claim:** Later Stage VC rounds account for 59% of all Quebec VC capital deployed 2020–2025 ($433M of $733M total), concentrated in just 23 deals — remove the top 3 deals and Quebec's 6-year total drops by ~$300M.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-sprint1-funding-2026
- **Implication:** Quebec's VC aggregate is highly misleading as a health metric — a handful of late-stage institutional rounds dominate the total, masking the thin early-stage market underneath.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

## Investor Composition

### MSG-FUNDING-06: State capital dominance — top investors are all public/institutional
- **Claim:** The six most active investors in Quebec startups by deal count (2020–2025) are all state or institutional: Investissement Québec (148 deals), Desjardins Capital (86), Anges Québec (65), BDC Capital (63), Fonds FTQ (44), and Fondaction (43) — private VC firms appear but with significantly lower volumes.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-sprint1-funding-2026
- **Implication:** Quebec's VC market is state-capital-architected; the absence of a strong independent VC tier means startups have limited options beyond institutional gatekeepers, reducing selection diversity and risk appetite.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15

### MSG-FUNDING-07: US investor withdrawal — near-zero by 2025
- **Claim:** US investor capital participation in Quebec deals dropped from ~36% of deployed capital in 2020 to approximately 1% in 2025, a faster and steeper decline than the national US VC pullback trend.
- **Confidence:** Medium
- **Evidence:** INTERNAL-sprint1-funding-2026
- **Implication:** Quebec's ecosystem is increasingly isolated from international growth capital exactly when global AI investment is accelerating — this structural gap may compound over the next 3–5 years if not addressed.
- **Last verified:** 2026-04-15
