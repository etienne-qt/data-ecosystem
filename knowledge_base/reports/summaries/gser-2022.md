# Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2022

## TL;DR
The 10th anniversary GSER emphasizes entrepreneurship percolating everywhere with fierce global competition. AI and cleantech gain prominence as defining sectors. Research covers 280+ cities and 3 million companies. Quebec's Mathieu Gervais (MEIE) participates in the Global Innovation Policy Forum, signaling the province's growing role in international ecosystem governance.

## Key Data Points

1. **[macro-trends]** COVID accelerated both startup growth and ecosystem importance; tech startups increasingly changing the world, not just disrupting markets.
2. **[entrepreneurship]** Competition on global leaderboard intensifying as entrepreneurship becomes accessible across geographies.
3. **[ai-sector]** AI identified as critical to startup competitiveness; roughly 40% of tech startups incorporating AI.
4. **[talent]** Talent dispersal from traditional hubs continues; AI tools enabling distributed innovation.
5. **[policy]** Government interest in entrepreneurship intensifying; leaders democratizing opportunities through startup ecosystems.
6. **[cleantech-sector]** Cleantech gains relevance with dedicated GSER sub-edition launched.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

- Montreal listed on page 251 in North America ecosystem pages
- Quebec City on page 263 in established ecosystems section
- Mathieu Gervais (Assistant Deputy Minister for Science and Innovation, Quebec) participates in Global Innovation Policy Forum (page 11)
- Indicates Quebec's growing participation in global ecosystem governance conversations

## Methodology & Limitations

- 10-year retrospective; 300+ partner organization network
- Research across 280+ cities on 3 million companies
- **Limitations:** Macro-level focus; limited detailed Quebec performance metrics in summary pages

## Relevance to Our Work

Shows systemic shift toward inclusive entrepreneurship; highlights AI as transformative force; identifies cleantech as emerging priority; Quebec government actively engaging in global policy conversations.
