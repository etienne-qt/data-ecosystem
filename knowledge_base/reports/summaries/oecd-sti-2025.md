# OECD Science, Technology and Innovation Outlook 2025

## TL;DR
This comprehensive OECD analysis reveals Canada's R&D intensity gap is widening at a critical moment. At 1.81% of GDP (second-lowest in G7), Canada's R&D growth of 1.8% annually significantly lags the OECD average of 3.2%. Government R&D budgets fell 1.9% in real terms globally in 2024, compounding pressure on Canadian innovation systems.

## Key Data Points
1. **Canada's R&D intensity:** 1.81% of GDP vs. 2.73% OECD average (second-lowest in G7)
2. **Growth gap:** Canada's GERD grew 1.8% annually (2011–2023) vs. OECD's 3.2%
3. **Total Canadian GERD:** $51.7B CAD in 2022, with Quebec contributing $12.7B (~24.5% of national total)
4. **University burden:** Canadian universities perform 35% of national GERD vs. 16% OECD average
5. **Government R&D:** OECD government budgets declined 1.9% in real terms (2024)
6. **AI leadership:** Canada was first country to adopt national AI strategy (2017)

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Quebec's research ecosystem performs approximately $12.7B in GERD annually, representing nearly one-quarter of Canada's total
- Major research institutions (McGill, UdeM, Polytechnique) carry disproportionate R&D burden relative to business sector
- Quebec universities exceed OECD norms in research intensity but this masks weakness in commercialization pathways
