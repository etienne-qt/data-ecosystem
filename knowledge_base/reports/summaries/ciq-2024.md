# Conseil de l'innovation du Québec — Vers un Québec innovant

## TL;DR
This government-mandated advisory report documents Quebec's 20-year stagnation in business R&D investment and proposes 13 recommendations across three pillars: inciting R&D investment, accelerating market entry, and protecting public investment. The report directly shaped Quebec's 2025-2026 budget and the new CRIC tax credit, demonstrating rare advisory-to-policy impact within a single budget cycle.

## Key Data Points
1. **Quebec business R&D investment stagnation** — Nearly 20-year slowdown with no material acceleration
2. **SME R&D impact** — SMEs conducting R&D over 8 years had 13% more assets, 25% higher revenue, and 27% greater profitability
3. **Government support measures deployed** — Approximately 10,000 direct and indirect financial measures annually
4. **SME utilization challenge** — Low take-up of government programs due to complexity and lack of specialized guidance
5. **CRIC tax credit** — New "Crédit d'impôt pour la recherche, l'innovation et la commercialisation" inspired by report recommendations, simplifying R&D tax credit regime
6. **Policy recommendations** — 13 total across 3 axes: inciting R&D investment, accelerating market entry, protecting public investment
7. **Support model transition** — Report proposes shift from subsidies to recyclable loans
8. **Report authorship** — Co-led by Université Laval rectrice and Quebec Chief Innovation Officer with international expert input (Max Planck Institute, CIRANO, University of Toronto)

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Quebec's R&D support infrastructure — 10,000+ direct and indirect financial measures — creates a complexity barrier that prevents many SMEs from accessing available support without dedicated specialists
- The report achieved direct policy impact within one budget cycle, with recommendations explicitly cited in Quebec's 2025-2026 budget
- Government support programs are underutilized, particularly among smaller firms lacking dedicated grant/tax-credit specialists
- SMEs with consistent R&D engagement (over 8 years) show material competitive advantage (25% higher revenue, 27% greater profitability), indicating high ROI on sustained R&D investment
- Report leadership included top academic and government innovation officials, lending credibility and enabling rapid policy uptake

## Methodology & Limitations
- Full PDF freely available
- Report based on analysis of Quebec business survey data, tax records, and international benchmarking
- Co-authored by senior academic and government innovation leadership
- International expert input from Max Planck Institute, CIRANO, and University of Toronto
- Proposes but does not quantify full economic impact of recommendations
