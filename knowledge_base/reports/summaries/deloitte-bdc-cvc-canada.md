# The State of Corporate Venture Capital (CVC) in Canada

## TL;DR
Canada's CVC ecosystem is maturing but significantly underperforming relative to its economic size. Only 6% of Canadian public companies earning over $1 billion annually participate in direct VC deals — compared to 40% in the US. Canadian CVCs demonstrated resilience during the 2022-2023 downturn (68 deals in 2023, exceeding pre-pandemic levels), but sector concentration in financial services/tech and preference for foreign deals over domestic investments limit ecosystem impact.

## Key Data Points

1. **[funding]** Canadian CVCs participated in 68 deals in 2023, exceeding pre-pandemic 2019 levels (54 deals), demonstrating sector momentum.
2. **[funding]** 47% of Canadian CVC deal activity in 2023 involved domestic startups, up from 36% in 2019.
3. **[funding]** Average deal size declined from $52 million in 2020 to $35 million in 2023, reflecting larger proportions of early-stage investments.
4. **[funding]** Seed and early-stage deals represented 31-53% of Canadian CVC investments between 2019-2023, showing strategic pivot.
5. **[macro-trends]** Only 6% of Canadian public companies earning over $1B annually engaged in direct VC deals in 2023, versus 40% in the US.
6. **[funding]** Financial services, media/telecom, and technology accounted for 88% of CVC activity; industrial/manufacturing and energy represented only 2% combined.
7. **[macro-trends]** Worldwide CVC participation in Canadian-headquartered VC deals declined from 18% (2019) to 12% (2023).
8. **[funding]** Canada's share of global CVC deal activity (1.0x GDP ratio) significantly lags the US (2.0x-2.2x) between 2021-2023.
9. **[funding]** Canadian-headquartered startups receive 76% of CVC participation from US-based CVC firms but only 39% from Canadian CVCs.
10. **[commercialization]** CVCs create three core value streams: expanded product offerings, innovation insights, and accelerated scale with reduced failure risk.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

The report does not provide Quebec-specific breakdowns. Geographic focus is limited to Canada-wide trends and international comparisons with the United States.

## Methodology & Limitations

- **Data Sources:** PitchBook (primary) + direct surveying of CVC funds
- **Scope:** Only Canadian corporations with formal CVC teams; excludes government and impact funds
- **Period:** 2019-2023 (through December 31, 2023)
- **Limitations:** Restricted to CVC funds with published deal activity; reporting bias toward larger transactions

## Relevance to Our Work

- **Q5.1** (VC sources): Identifies CVC as underutilized capital source in Canada with massive growth potential
- **Capital gap:** 6% vs 40% participation rate reveals structural gap Quebec ecosystem could help address
- **Sector diversification:** 88% concentration in fin/tech/media leaves cleantech, healthtech, and industrial sectors underserved by CVC
