# Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2017

## TL;DR
The 2017 GSER establishes foundational frameworks for assessing startup ecosystems globally, introducing a lifecycle model categorizing ecosystems into four stages (Opening, Scale, Transition, Maturity). The report identifies key metrics for ecosystem health with focus on how policy, mentorship, talent, funding, and network density drive entrepreneurial success across geographies.

## Key Data Points

1. **[macro-trends]** Ecosystem Lifecycle Model introduced with four stages (Opening through Maturity), each with characteristic conditions and success factors.
2. **[entrepreneurship]** Global rankings framework established for comparing 30+ major startup ecosystems worldwide across funding flows, talent quality, and exit valuations.
3. **[funding]** Different ecosystem stages require different capital structures; early-stage ecosystems need government support and angel funding; mature ecosystems attract institutional VC.
4. **[talent]** Founder quality, technical talent availability, and talent retention correlate directly with ecosystem success; brain drain identified as significant challenge for emerging ecosystems.
5. **[entrepreneurship]** Network density (connections between founders, investors, mentors, service providers) determines ecosystem velocity and opportunity flow.
6. **[policy]** Government policies around immigration, taxation, IP protection, and regulatory burden significantly influence ecosystem competitiveness.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

The 2017 report does not provide detailed Quebec-specific analysis, though the framework establishes metrics applicable to assessing the Montreal/Quebec ecosystem.

## Methodology & Limitations

- Mixed-methods research combining quantitative data (funding flows, exit values, company counts) with qualitative assessments (expert interviews, ecosystem observations)
- Data collection focuses on major hubs with visible funding activity
- **Limitations:** Potential underestimation of informal networks in emerging ecosystems; variations in data availability across regions

## Relevance to Our Work

Establishes the conceptual framework and classification system used in subsequent GSER reports. Understanding the ecosystem lifecycle model is essential for contextualizing Montreal's position.
