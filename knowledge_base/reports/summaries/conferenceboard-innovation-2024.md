# Conference Board of Canada: 2024 Innovation Report Card

## TL;DR
Canada receives an overall "C" grade, ranking 15th of 20 peer nations on innovation. The persistent "Innovation Paradox" is quantified: Canada excels in foundational metrics (top-cited papers, ease of entrepreneurship, new firm density) but scores second-to-last on venture capital investment and demonstrates negligible commercialization of research into economic growth. Canada ranks dead last in OECD for long-term per capita GDP growth.

## Key Data Points
1. **Overall ranking:** 15th of 20 peer countries; "C" grade
2. **Grade distribution:** 13 "D" grades, 2 "C" grades, 6 "B" grades, 0 "A" grades
3. **Below-average scores:** 14 of 21 indicators
4. **Top performers:** Switzerland (1st); Canada excels in top-cited papers, ease of entrepreneurship, new firm density
5. **Weak points:** Second-to-last on venture capital investment; dead last in OECD for long-term per capita GDP growth
6. **Paradox:** Strong research foundation with weak commercialization outcomes

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Conference Board analysis does not provide Quebec-specific provincial breakdown
- University research strength (McGill, UdeM, Polytechnique) contributes to Canada's top-cited papers ranking
- Weak VC investment ranking directly impacts Quebec's ability to commercialize research from world-class institutions
- "Innovation Paradox" highlights structural gap between Quebec's research excellence and startup ecosystem capital availability

## Methodology & Limitations
- Peer comparison: Canada vs. 19 comparable developed nations
- Data sources: OECD, IMD, national statistical agencies
- Time period: Analysis based on 2023–2024 data; report published April 2024
