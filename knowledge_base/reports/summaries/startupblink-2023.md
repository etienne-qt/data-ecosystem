# StartupBlink Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2023

## TL;DR
Canada maintains #4 global ranking across the 2020-2023 period, demonstrating consistent ecosystem strength. AI sector emergence reshapes capital allocation and talent competition globally. Montreal gains recognition as competitive AI hub leveraging MILA and university research centers. Remote-work models become permanent structural feature. Exit activity and commercialization metrics gain prominence in assessment.

## Key Data Points

1. **[entrepreneurship]** Canada #4 globally (consistent across 2020-2023), demonstrating stability through multiple economic cycles.
2. **[ai-sector]** AI becomes central to ecosystem analysis with dedicated rankings and capital tracking.
3. **[funding]** Increasing capital concentration in AI, healthtech, and specialized sectors.
4. **[exits]** Tracking of successful exits, acquisitions, and IPOs becomes more prominent in methodology.
5. **[talent]** Competition for AI/ML technical talent intensifies across ecosystems.
6. **[macro-trends]** Remote-first models become permanent structural feature rather than temporary pandemic response.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

- Montreal gains recognition as significant AI hub with strong research-to-startup pipeline
- University-based AI research (MILA and related institutions) creates competitive advantage
- Quebec healthtech ecosystem continues maturing with established funding and exit pathways
- Montreal increasingly recognized for cleantech innovation
- Montreal's bilingual talent and cultural position noted as ecosystem differentiator

## Methodology & Limitations

- Four-year longitudinal analysis enabling trend identification
- AI sector receives enhanced analytical depth
- Expanded focus on exits and commercialization
- **Limitations:** AI definitional variations; ecosystem partner data relies on self-reporting; rapid market changes

## Relevance to Our Work

Four-year trend analysis provides excellent context for Quebec's trajectory. AI positioning particularly relevant given Montreal's advantages. Consistent #4 national ranking alongside sectoral specialization helps position Quebec's opportunities.
