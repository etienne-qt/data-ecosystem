# ISQ Enquête sur l'innovation et les stratégies d'entreprise 2022

## TL;DR
Quebec's business innovation rate declined from 77.9% (2017–2019) to 70% (2020–2022), driven primarily by pandemic disruptions. However, digital and IP-intensive activities bucked the trend, with software development and IP protection rising significantly.

## Key Data Points
1. **Overall innovation rate** — Dropped from 77.9% to 70.0% (−7.9 percentage points)
2. **Product innovation decline** — Fell from 51.3% to 45.2%
3. **Business process innovation** — Decreased from 71.0% to 60.5%
4. **Software/database development growth** — Rose from 19.9% to 24.7%
5. **IP-related activities** — Increased from 9.0% to 11.9%
6. **IP protection adoption among innovators** — 20.7% filed IP vs. 3.5% of non-innovators (6× difference)
7. **Export rate** — 26.8% of enterprises exported
8. **Import rate** — 37.5% of enterprises imported
9. **Foreign-subsidiary rate** — 5.9% (indicating relatively insular business activity)

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- The pandemic caused broad innovation decline across traditional categories (product, process)
- Digital and IP-intensive activities proved resilient and grew during the 2020–2022 period
- Government program utilization increased, likely reflecting pandemic-era accessibility of financial support
- Quebec has a low foreign-subsidiary rate, signaling relatively insular business activity and limited foreign investment attraction
- Export and import rates suggest moderate international engagement compared to other provinces

## Methodology & Limitations
- Based on Statistics Canada's Survey of Innovation and Business Strategy (SIBS) 2022
- ISQ funded an enhanced Quebec sample in collaboration with MEIE, MESS, and other partners
- Innovation concepts follow the Oslo Manual 2018 framework
- Cross-cycle comparisons limited by methodological revisions between survey periods
