# Mila Impact Report 2024-2025

## TL;DR
Mila, the world's largest academic deep learning lab, surpassed 1,200 researchers with 42 new faculty (24% growth) and 140+ affiliated professors across 7 Quebec universities. The institute published 700+ peer-reviewed articles and launched 53 startups (12 new in one year), demonstrating a robust research-to-venture pipeline. 382 AI adoption projects directly impacted 227 Quebec organizations.

## Key Data Points
1. **AI Talent Pipeline** – 1,200+ student researchers; 42 new faculty (24% growth); 140+ affiliated professors from 7 Quebec universities
2. **Research Excellence** – 700+ peer-reviewed articles; 180+ accepted at NeurIPS 2024, ICLR 2025, ICML 2025
3. **Active Research Portfolio** – 650+ active research projects; $9M+ in scholarships
4. **Commercialization** – 53 total startups (12 new in one year); 382 AI adoption projects impacting 227 organizations
5. **Ecosystem Growth** – 176 partner organizations (49 new); Entrepreneurship Lab officially recognized by Quebec's Ministry of Economy in 2025

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Mila community represents the world's largest academic deep learning research concentration, anchoring Quebec's position in AI
- 382 AI adoption projects demonstrate strong knowledge transfer beyond academia to Quebec SMEs
- Entrepreneurship Lab official recognition signals government-industry alignment on AI commercialization

## Methodology & Limitations
- Data sourced from Mila's first-party institutional reporting (high reliability)
- Web-based interactive format; metrics as of October 2025
- Startup count reflects cumulative pipeline; new startups (12) represent annual growth rate
