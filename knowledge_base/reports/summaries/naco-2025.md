# NACO 2025 Annual Report on Angel Investing in Canada

## TL;DR
Canadian angel investment rebounded 27% to $146.2M in 2024, with median deal sizes jumping 50% to $129,520. However, totals remain below 2021 pre-pandemic levels, and NACO estimates reported figures represent only a fraction of actual angel activity. Press release data only; full report requires NACO membership/purchase.

## Key Data Points
1. **Total angel investment:** $146.2M in 2024 (+27.2% YoY)
2. **Median investment per company:** $129,520 (+50% from $83,635 in 2023)
3. **Angel networks surveyed:** 38 Canadian angel networks
4. **NACO membership:** >4,000 investors across >100 organizations
5. **Cumulative member investment:** >$1.8B in >2,000 ventures
6. **Women angels:** ~35% of membership (slight decrease)
7. **Key gaps:** Investment below 2021 levels; reported figures described as "significant underestimate" of true angel activity
8. **Policy recommendation:** $450M early-stage matching fund; 30% National Investment Tax Credit

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Anges Québec is the largest Quebec angel group: 200+ members, >$171M invested in ~200 companies since 2008
- Quebec lacks direct angel investor tax credit unlike BC, Alberta, and Nova Scotia
- Anges Québec launched Elevia Fund for pre-seed and seed co-investment
- Tax credit gap puts Quebec at disadvantage for early-stage deal flow relative to peer provinces

## Methodology & Limitations
- Data sourced from press release and secondary reporting only
- Full report behind membership paywall; press release provides partial disclosure
- Survey covers 38 formal angel networks; NACO acknowledges reported figures underestimate total angel activity
- Women angel representation declined slightly (35% vs. prior periods)
- Gender diversity: 65% male-owned, 17% female-owned (among SMEs surveyed elsewhere)

## Relevance to Our Work
- **Q5.1 (Early-Stage Capital):** Angel investment rebound (+27%) partially offsets seed-stage VC decline but remains below 2021 baseline
- **Q6.1 (Policy):** Tax credit gap disadvantages Quebec relative to BC, Alberta, Nova Scotia for deal flow
- **Q3.1 (Talent/Diversity):** Women angels at ~35% indicates gender gap persisting in early-stage capital access
- **Q2.1 (Innovation):** Median deal size growth (+50%) suggests shift toward higher-confidence rounds; early-stage companies may face funding gaps
