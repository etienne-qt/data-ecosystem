# Global Startup Ecosystem Report 2019

## TL;DR
The 2019 GSER provides updated ecosystem rankings with enhanced analysis of performance drivers: talent quality, policy responsiveness, and capital flow patterns. The report documents continued geographic diversification of startup activity while highlighting persistent competitive advantages of established hubs and challenges of ecosystem replication in new regions.

## Key Data Points

1. **[macro-trends]** Ecosystems with strong international linkages show superior outcomes in talent attraction, capital access, and knowledge flows.
2. **[talent]** Founder capability and team talent quality remain strongest predictors of venture success; ecosystems with higher team quality achieve better exits.
3. **[funding]** Venture capital increasingly concentrates in proven ecosystems; new ecosystems struggle to achieve proportional capital access despite growing company creation.
4. **[policy]** Ecosystems with rapid government responsiveness (visas, regulatory sandboxes, IP protection) gain competitive advantages in multi-year competition.
5. **[commercialization]** Time from formation to first revenue varies significantly by ecosystem; correlates with mentor access, talent quality, and policy support.
6. **[exits]** Mature exit markets (M&A acquirers, strategic investors) concentrate in larger ecosystems; new ecosystems struggle to provide exit opportunities.

## Quebec-Specific Findings

No detailed Quebec-specific analysis in pages 1-20, though the emphasis on global network connectivity and talent quality provides framework for assessing Montreal's position within North American networks.

## Methodology & Limitations

- Comprehensive data across 30+ ecosystems with refined metrics for capital flows, talent movements, and exit patterns
- Three years of longitudinal data enabling trajectory analysis
- **Limitations:** Focus on formal venture activity; challenges capturing emerging sector dynamics due to rapid change

## Relevance to Our Work

Findings on network connectivity, talent quality, and commercialization velocity directly applicable to assessing Montreal's competitive positioning.
