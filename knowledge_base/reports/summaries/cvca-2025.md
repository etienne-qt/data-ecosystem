# CVCA Year-End 2025 Canadian VC & PE Market Overview

## TL;DR
Canadian VC totaled $8.0B across 571 deals in 2025, representing a 6% decline in capital and 12% drop in deal count. Only 29 exits occurred—all M&A, zero IPOs—worth $358M disclosed value, while capital concentration reached new heights with just 26 megadeals capturing 66% of all VC funding.

## Key Data Points
1. **Capital Flow & Deals:** $8.0B across 571 deals (excl. secondaries); $9.3B including secondaries; 6% YoY capital decline, 12% deal count drop
2. **Capital Concentration:** 26 megadeals (>$50M) captured ~66% of all capital
3. **Sector Performance:** ICT dominated at $5.06B (63%); life sciences fell 47% to $837M; cleantech dropped from $1.2B to $660M
4. **Venture Debt:** Record $1.40B across 69 deals
5. **Exit Market:** Only 29 exits—all M&A, zero IPOs—worth $358M disclosed value
6. **US Investor Participation:** Fell to 25% of deals, down from 32%
7. **Quebec VC:** $1.0B across 122 deals; ~12.5% of national capital, ~21% of deal count

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Quebec recorded $1.0B in VC across 122 deals in 2025, ranking third by capital (after Ontario $4.6B and BC $1.4B)
- Captured ~12.5% of national VC dollars but ~21% of deal count, reflecting smaller average deal sizes than national norm
- Exit market freeze affects Quebec's ability to recycle capital domestically

## Methodology & Limitations
- Data from CVCA Intelligence platform, Canada's primary private capital database
- Combines member surveys, public disclosures, and third-party sources
- Figures subject to revision; angel financing excluded from VC totals
- Full PDF requires CVCA Intelligence access; analysis based on landing-page summary + BetaKit secondary coverage
