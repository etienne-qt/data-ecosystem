# PitchBook-NVCA Venture Monitor Q4 2025

## TL;DR
US venture capital surged to $339.4B across 16,709 deals in 2025—the second-highest annual total on record—driven entirely by AI dominance. AI/ML captured 65.6% of all VC deal value ($222B), up from 47.2% in 2024. The report contains no Canada-specific coverage but presents critical context for Quebec's competitive position as capital concentrates in AI-focused mega-rounds.

## Key Data Points
1. **US VC funding:** $339.4B across 16,709 deals in 2025 (second-highest annual total after 2021)
2. **AI/ML dominance:** $222B captured 65.6% of all US VC deal value (up from 47.2% in 2024, 10% in 2015)
3. **US exits:** $297.8B (up 92.7% YoY) with 62 IPOs generating $119.4B
4. **Fundraising:** $66.1B (lowest since 2018)
5. **Dry powder:** $311.2B available
6. **Global VC deal value:** ~$512B
7. **Enterprise AI spending:** Scaled from $1.7B (2023) to $37B (2025)

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- The report contains no Canada-specific sections
- Canadian VC fundraising context from supplementary sources: 21 Canadian VC funds raised ~$2.1B CAD in 2025 (worst since 2016)
- Top 5 Canadian VC funds captured 83% of capital raised, indicating extreme concentration
- Massive AI capital surge in US creates both opportunity (leveraging Montreal's AI talent) and risk (talent and company gravity toward US)
