# Montréal International — Greater Montréal, Launchpad for Emerging Ventures 2025

## TL;DR
Greater Montreal is established as a top emerging-venture destination with $2.7B in supported FDI across 59 projects creating 4,790 jobs (average salary $105,439). The region hosts 627,100 STEM jobs, 48,000+ AI-skilled workers, and 160,000 IT workers (Canada's 2nd-largest hub), with 500,000+ post-secondary students. Montreal offers a 37% cost advantage for software development vs. top 20 North American metros, with software engineer salaries at $106K USD vs. $190K in San Francisco. 30 accelerators/incubators support early-stage ventures.

## Key Data Points
1. **FDI Attraction** – $2.7B supported by Montréal International in 2024 across 59 projects; 4,790 jobs created/maintained; average salary $105,439
2. **Talent Supply** – 627,100 STEM jobs; 48,000+ AI-skilled workers; 160,000 IT workers; 500,000+ post-secondary students (60% in Greater Montreal)
3. **Cost Competitiveness** – 37% cost advantage for software development vs. top 20 North American metros; software engineer salary $106K USD (Montreal) vs. $190K (San Francisco)
4. **Operating Cost Comparison** – 500-person tech company: $37.5M annual (Montreal) vs. $81M (San Francisco)
5. **Ecosystem Infrastructure** – 30 accelerators/incubators
6. **FDI Origin (2024)** – US 33%; Switzerland 25%; France 21%

## Quebec-Specific Findings
- Montreal ranks among top 20 North American metros for tech operations cost and talent pool depth
- AI-skilled worker base (48,000+) positions Montreal as a global AI hub, supporting both startups and established firms
- High concentration of post-secondary students (500,000+ in Quebec, 60% in Greater Montreal) ensures continuous talent pipeline
- Nearshoring advantage through cost and proximity to US markets, with 33% of 2024 FDI from US
- Reinvestment projects increased 25% year-over-year, indicating stable investor confidence

## Methodology & Limitations
- Data sourced from full PDF accessed (Montréal International, 2025)
- FDI metrics reflect supported projects; actual capital deployment and timelines may vary by project stage
- AI-skilled workers counted via LinkedIn data; reporting may overestimate or underestimate actual available talent
- Cost comparisons based on software development sector benchmark; other sectors may have different cost profiles
- Salary data reflects market averages; individual variation significant
